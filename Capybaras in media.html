Capybaras in media

Between August and September of 2021, capybaras became a trending topic on the internet. Several memes were created surrounding a video of a capybara sitting on a passenger's seat while the song "After Party" by Don Toliver (2020) played throughout the video. Their chill personalities have won over so many people's hearts and I wouldn't blame them, who wouldn't obssess over a cute, big rodent that couldn't care less about whatever's happening around them.

But this hype around capybaras isn't something new though. Capybaras in Japan were the hype by around 2008! They first arrived to Japan during the 1960s and were kept in zoos, making them great for interactive exhibits, due to their nature. In 1980, the Nagasaki Bio Park was built and by 2008, a capybara bath facility was added. This facility consisted of little baths filled with warm water and fruit and capybaras that are free to pet and just chill by the water. At this time, capybaras were already wildly popular in Jpana due to a cartoon character called "Kapibara-san", so the addition of the capybara bath facility in the Nagasaki Bio Park added to its popularity. You can often see memes and pictures of capybaras with oranges balancing on their heads or peeking through the water surrounded by fruit.
