Capybaras: What are they?

Well, probably one of the most wholesome animals ever. These beaver looking creatures, although not from the same family, are the largest rodents in the world, weighing 27-79 Kg. Their fur varies from coffee brown to grey-ish brown with a yellow-ish brown on their underside. Capybaras have short legs with four toes on their front legs and three toes on their back legs. They're mammals that hang out in groups of up to 20 members of their family. Their main predators are the jaguar and the ocelot. Capybaras are often considered a pest in agriculture and is often hunted for their meat and skin.

Where can I find them?

They're found in most of South America, mainly in countries such as Panama, Peru, Venezuela, Brazil, Uruguay, and Columbia. They often hang out in grasslands and near water and sleep under thick vegetation.
